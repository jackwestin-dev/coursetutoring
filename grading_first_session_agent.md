# SOP: Session 1 Grading Rubric (Equal-Weight 1–10 Scale)

## Purpose
This SOP defines how the grading agent evaluates Session 1 tutoring transcripts
using a **1–10 scoring scale per category**, with **equal weight** across categories.

The goal is to assess **quality, preparedness, and instructional effectiveness**
without penalizing tutors for natural session flow or stylistic differences.

---

## Scoring Overview

Each category is scored **1–10**.  
All categories carry **equal importance**.

| Category |
|--------|
| Preparation & Planning Readiness |
| Study Plan Construction Quality |
| Personalization & Load Calibration |
| Strategy Portion Execution |
| Clarity & Student Buy-In |

---

## 1. Preparation & Planning Readiness (1–10)

**What This Measures**  
Whether the tutor arrived with context and direction, even if some details were
confirmed live.

### Score Anchors
- **9–10**  
  Tutor demonstrates clear awareness of:
  - Student test date (identified or confirmed)
  - Baseline score or relative strengths/weaknesses
  - Topics already covered by live courses
  - Below-average or priority topics

- **7–8**  
  Tutor identifies most elements but clarifies some details during the session

- **5–6**  
  Tutor frames goals and test date but shows limited baseline analysis

- **3–4**  
  Tutor appears mostly reactive with minimal prior context

- **1–2**  
  No evidence of preparation or planning

**Reasoning**  
Preparation can be demonstrated through framing and questioning, not memorization.

---

## 2. Study Plan Construction Quality (1–10)

**What This Measures**  
Whether the tutor builds a **usable and structured study plan**.

### Core Indicators
- Practice exam schedule
- AAMC material sequencing or deadlines
- Weekly checklist or priorities
- Daily tasks for **Week 1**

### Score Anchors
- **9–10**  
  Clear, structured plan including all core indicators

- **7–8**  
  Strong structure with some elements kept high-level

- **5–6**  
  Plan exists but timelines or specificity are limited

- **3–4**  
  Mostly general advice with minimal structure

- **1–2**  
  No actionable plan presented

**Reasoning**  
Direction and clarity matter more than granularity.

---

## 3. Personalization & Load Calibration (1–10)

**What This Measures**  
Whether the plan realistically fits the student’s life and constraints.

### Score Anchors
- **9–10**  
  Tutor actively adapts plan based on:
  - Availability
  - Work/school obligations
  - Burnout or pacing considerations

- **7–8**  
  Tutor asks about availability and adjusts workload generally

- **5–6**  
  Minimal personalization; plan mostly generic

- **3–4**  
  Time constraints acknowledged but not meaningfully applied

- **1–2**  
  No discussion of time, capacity, or constraints

**Reasoning**  
A realistic plan is more valuable than an aggressive one.

---

## 4. Strategy Portion Execution (1–10)

**What This Measures**  
Whether the tutor teaches **how to think**, not just what to study.

### Strategy Indicators
- Teach-back moments
- Feedback on student reasoning
- Discussion of challenging or representative questions
- Strategy refinement during the session

### Score Anchors
- **9–10**  
  Tutor consistently checks understanding and adjusts strategies live

- **7–8**  
  Strategy taught with some feedback and application

- **5–6**  
  Strategy discussed but mostly tutor-led

- **3–4**  
  Strategy mentioned briefly or abstractly

- **1–2**  
  No meaningful strategy instruction

**Reasoning**  
Strategy instruction is the core value of tutoring.

---

## 5. Clarity & Student Buy-In (1–10)

**What This Measures**  
Whether the student leaves knowing exactly what to do next.

### Score Anchors
- **9–10**  
  Student clearly verbalizes next steps and assignments

- **7–8**  
  Tutor summarizes plan; student confirms understanding

- **5–6**  
  Plan exists but clarity is not explicitly checked

- **3–4**  
  Session ends with unclear next steps

- **1–2**  
  No recap or direction provided

**Reasoning**  
Understanding and confidence matter more than documentation polish.

---

## Required Session 1 Documentation (Contextual Requirement)

Expected inclusions:
- Exam schedule
- AAMC deadlines or sequencing
- Below-average topics (excluding course-covered content)
- Weekly checklist
- Daily tasks for Week 1
- Strategy notes
- Tentative next session date

**Grading Rule**
- Missing items reduce relevant category scores
- No automatic failure for formatting or naming differences

---

## Final Evaluation Output

Agent should return:
- A **1–10 score for each category**
- 1–2 sentence justification per category
- A brief overall assessment:
  - `Strong Session`
  - `Adequate / On Track`
  - `Needs Improvement`
  - `Review Required`

---

## Agent Guidance
- Score holistically
- Favor demonstrated intent over exact phrasing
- When uncertain, score conservatively

---

## Core Principle
This rubric evaluates **effective tutoring and student clarity**, not perfect paperwork.
